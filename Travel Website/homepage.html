VISIT TENERIFE

WHERE TO PLAY

El Teide

At 3,718 meters, it is Spain’s highest peak and its two ecosystems make the climb an experience full of contrasts. With local guides you get an in-depth experience and the mountain refuge is the spot for superb dawn views.

Los Gigantes

Los Gigantes is a resort town in the Santiago del Teide. Its main feature are the giant rock formations, Acantilados de Los Gigantes, that rise from the sea to a height of 500-850 metres after which the town has been named.

Cueva del Viento

The Cueva del Viento was shaped by lava flows from Pico Viejo, a volcanic cone located alongside Mount Teide. A volcanic tunnel is a cavity that forms inside a lava flow. As lava flows and comes into contact with air, the surface hardens.

WHAT TO EAT

Papas Arrugadas

"Wrinkled potatoes" are a popular side dish consisting of small potatoes boiled in salt water until soft, leaving the potatoes with wrinkles and with a salty crust.

Sancocho

This fish dish is made with parna, a type of bass, boiled with plain or sweet potatoes and served with a spicy sauce, normally mojo picón or mojo verde.

Bienmesabe

This tasty almond-cream dessert is made of honey, ground almonds, cinnamon, and egg yolks. This traditional dessert pairs well with fresh coffee.

WHERE TO STAY

Hotel Jacaranda

A 2-minute walk from Faabé Plaza bus stop, this relaxed hotel is a 7-minute walk from Fañabé beach and 10 km from Las Águilas Jungle Park. Starting at $599.

Melia Jardina del Teide

Within the Costa Adeje Resort, this upscale hotel is 1 km from Playa del Duque beach and 3 km from Aqualand water park. Starting at $699.

Alborada Ocean Club

This laid-back resort is a 1-minute walk from Ten Bel bus station, 11 km from Amarilla Golf and Country Club and 15 km from Las Águilas. Starting at $799.

Allegro Isora

Set on 2 hectares of leafy grounds, this relaxed all-inclusive resort is a 9-minute walk from the black sands of La Arena Beach. Starting at $899.
